Kadri Manjunath Temple
Location: Kadri Hills, Mangaluru, Karnataka, India
Google Maps: https://maps.app.goo.gl/xRksAR5wR3ZoWwFZ9

Description:
Kadri Manjunath Temple is one of the oldest temples in Mangaluru, dedicated to Lord Manjunatha (a form of Lord Shiva). The temple dates back to around the 10th–11th century and is known for its unique blend of Buddhist and Shaiva traditions, ancient bronze idols, and sacred natural water tanks believed to have spiritual significance. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}


Mangaladevi Temple
Location: Bolar (Mangaladevi Area), Mangaluru, Karnataka, India
Google Maps: https://maps.app.goo.gl/38Rr31AwTCQG335z8

Description:
Mangaladevi Temple is a historic temple dedicated to Goddess Mangaladevi, after whom the city of Mangaluru gets its name. Believed to date back to the 9th century during the Alupa dynasty, the temple is an important cultural and religious center and attracts many devotees during Navaratri. Scenes from the Kannada film “Garuda Gamana Vrishabha Vahana” were shot near this temple area. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}


Gokarnanatheshwara Temple (Kudroli Temple)
Location: Kudroli, Mangaluru, Karnataka, India
Google Maps: https://maps.app.goo.gl/UdJUVgvtijCzUR7k8

Description:
Gokarnanatheshwara Temple, also known as Kudroli Temple, is dedicated to Lord Shiva (Gokarnanatha) and was consecrated by social reformer Sri Narayana Guru in 1912. The temple is famous for its grand Dravidian-style architecture and vibrant Dasara celebrations, making it one of the most visited temples in Mangaluru. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
